Well, it feels like I'm not hugry anymore. And that turns out to be a problem.

When I was a teenager, adults told me what my parents should do. I resisted, I wrinkled my face, I got distracted by all sorts of little things, but I did it.
I had classes and exams, I had a chance to go to a good university, I had something to do, and knew what would happen.

When I first moved away from my parents, I was trying to figure out how to live. How to get my first job, what foods I could cook quickly and easily, how I could rent my own place and move out on my own.

Then, I think I got used to it, and realized that I clearly didn't have enough money to live the full life I wanted to live.
That became my goal for a few years - to become a professional in a job that I enjoy so that I could afford to live comfortably.

And now I seem to be working at a job that I'm not ashamed of. I get a good enough salary by the standards of a poor family I grew up in, I moved to the capital, I can afford a good apartment, food, health care.

I will definitely continue to raise my salary, just as I will continue to learn cooking.
But now it seems that lack of money no longer prevents me from doing other things.
I can make time for something truly worthwhile, but I don'teven know what.
Poverty and lack of skills used to bite at my heels, I used to run from them and due to that I realized my path.

Now I seem to have solved my problems, and that's a problem.

I finished the corridor tutorial, went out into the open world and... there are no quests.
Somehow I need to now decide what kind of person I am in general, and what personally worthwhile things I can do.
