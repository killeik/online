Well, it feels like I'm not hugry anymore. And that turns out to be a problem.

When I was a teenager, adults told me what I should do. I resisted, wrinkled face, got distracted by all sorts of little things, but I did what they told me to do.
I had classes and exams, had a chance to go to a good university, had something to do, and knew what would happen.

When I moved away from my parents, I was trying to figure out how to live. How to get first job, what foods can be to cook, how to rent appartments and move out on my own.

Then, when I got used to living by myself, and realized that I clearly didn't have enough money to live the full life I wanted to live.
That became my goal for a few years - to become a professional in a job that I enjoy to afford comfortable life.

And now I seem to be working at good enough job, and get paid good enough salary by the standards of a poor family I grew up in. I moved to the capital, can afford a good enough apartment, food, health care.

I will definitely continue to raise my salary, just like I didn't stopped to thing about better appartment/food/etc.
But now it seems that lack of money no longer prevents me from doing other things.
I can make time for something truly worthwhile, but I don't even know what.
Poverty and lack of skills used to bite at my heels, I used to run from them and due to that I realized my path.

Now I seem to have solved my problems, and that's a problem.

Somehow I need to now decide what kind of person I am in general, and what personally worthwhile things I can do.
